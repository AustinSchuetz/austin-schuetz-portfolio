Meridian — a live trading system built to be trusted.

Strategy research, backtesting, and live execution against Interactive Brokers — designed, built, and operated as a two-year engagement with a solo trading principal.

The brief

A solo trading principal was running strategy research out of spreadsheets and a broker terminal. He needed one system that could research, backtest, and execute live — without a team to babysit it, and without ever placing an order he couldn't explain.

The approach

Reliability was treated as a design problem, not an ops chore. Backtests run through the exact code path that trades live, so a strategy that survives research behaves identically in production. Every order, fill, and rejection lands in an event-sourced log the dashboard replays on demand.

BACKTESTS SHARE THE LIVE EXECUTION CODE PATH
FAIL-CLOSED RISK CHECKS ON EVERY ORDER
EVENT-SOURCED ORDER LOG, REPLAYABLE FROM THE DASHBOARD
CLAUDE REVIEWS OVERNIGHT RUNS & FLAGS ANOMALIES PRE-OPEN

The outcome

Two years in, Meridian trades through Interactive Brokers every market day. The morning routine is a five-minute read of the Claude-written overnight brief instead of an hour of spreadsheet archaeology — and the engagement continues on a retainer basis.
